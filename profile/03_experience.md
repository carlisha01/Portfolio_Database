**Long-term family business involvement, with progressively increasing responsibility**

Carla's connection to Rocxi Beach began long before her transition into business analysis. The restaurant gave her early exposure to clients, service, pressure, teamwork, restaurant operations, and the reality of running a business where every detail affects both the customer experience and the financial outcome.

Over time, her role evolved from operational support into a more analytical and transformation-focused contribution. She began identifying opportunities to improve how the restaurant uses data, manages customer feedback, understands sales, and structures information for decision-making.

### Restaurant Operations and Customer Experience

Carla has worked directly within the restaurant environment, supporting day-to-day operations and learning how service businesses function under real pressure. She understands table flow, customer expectations, reservations, service rhythm, team coordination, menu communication, and guest satisfaction.

Because Rocxi Beach has many long-time clients and family-connected customers, Carla has also developed a strong sensitivity to personalised service — understanding that hospitality is not only about efficiency, but also about emotional intelligence, memory, detail, and making people feel recognised.

**New Year's Eve Personalised Experience:** Carla designed and printed personalised menus for each reservation and wrote individual notes for fourteen tables made up of long-time clients and family friends. This project combined operational preparation, guest experience design, personalisation, and emotional attention to detail.

### KPI Tracking and Dashboard Work

Carla has worked on building structured visibility around restaurant performance through dashboards and KPI tracking using Looker Studio and Power BI.

**Focus areas:**
- Sales trends and peak service hours

The objective is not simply visual reports, but decision-making tools: identifying when the restaurant is busiest, where service pressure increases, which products perform best, which feedback patterns repeat, and where management attention should go.

Carla has worked on analysing customer feedback from sources such as CoverManager, online reviews, and post-visit survey data, using AI-assisted methods to categorise large amounts of unstructured feedback into useful business insights.

**Feedback categories tracked:**

- Food quality and dish-specific feedback
- Atmosphere and price perception
- Reservation experience
- Most liked and least liked dishes

This allows management to detect patterns rather than reacting only to isolated reviews — turning qualitative comments into structured operational intelligence.

Rocxi Beach uses CoverManager for reservations, customer profiles, and post-visit communication. Carla has worked on improving how the restaurant uses available customer data.

**Areas explored:**
- Customer segmentation and best-client identification
- Reservation data analysis

- Feedback loops and satisfaction tracking

### Gmail to Google Drive Automation for CoverManager Reports

Carla built and refined a Gmail-to-Drive automation using Google Apps Script to manage recurring CoverManager reports. The problem was that CoverManager sends recurring reports via email with downloadable CSV links — manually managing these files was creating friction and risking lost or disorganised data.

**Technical details:**
- Gmail label processing: `RocxiBeach/GuardarDrive` / done label: `Procesado`
- Gmail query logic to exclude already-processed messages
- Google Apps Script with Drive API
- Weekly trigger planning
- CSV handling, naming logic, de-duplication
- Folder schema for raw-data storage

**Reports automated:** client exports, best-client reports, cancellation data, satisfaction stats, valuation groups, reservation statistics.

### Supplier, Product, and Cost Analysis

Carla has worked on matching products sold in the restaurant with products purchased from suppliers to support profitability analysis — comparing sales files with supplier purchase files, identifying name matches, categorising products, and preparing data for margin analysis.

**Business questions addressed:**
- Which products generate the most revenue and best margin?
- Which low-cost, high-margin, high-volume products should be prioritised?
- How do supplier costs connect to menu items?
- Which product families need deeper cost control?
