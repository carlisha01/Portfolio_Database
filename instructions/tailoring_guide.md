# Tailoring Guide — How to Adapt Carla's Profile to Different Role Types

This guide explains the thinking behind tailoring, so you can review and adjust documents Claude generates — or brief Claude more precisely when needed.

---

## The Core Principle

Carla's profile has a strong through-line: operational experience + analytical and digital skills. But different roles need different parts of that story to come first.

The angle files in `positioning/role_angles/` pre-decide this for each role type. But understanding the logic helps you review quality and catch mismatches.

---

## By Role Type

### Business Analyst
**Lead with:** Revenue management internship (Majestic) + Dashboard project + Feedback Analysis project
**Frame as:** Someone who structures data and translates it into decisions
**De-emphasise:** Frontline service details, hospitality atmosphere
**Key language:** KPIs, data structuring, reporting, insight generation, process mapping

### Operations Analyst
**Lead with:** Rocxi Beach operational + analytical work + Automation project + Digital Transformation Roadmap
**Frame as:** Someone who understands how operations work and can measure and improve them
**De-emphasise:** Luxury hospitality atmosphere
**Key language:** Operational efficiency, process improvement, workflow design, performance monitoring

### CRM Operations
**Lead with:** CoverManager CRM work + CRM Segmentation Strategy + Automation project
**Frame as:** Someone who treats CRM as a relationship and revenue tool, not just a database
**De-emphasise:** Pure analytics / dashboard angle
**Key language:** Customer segmentation, CRM data, personalised communication, customer lifecycle, retention

### Digital Transformation
**Lead with:** EAE Digital Transformation Roadmap + Automation project (Google Apps Script) + AI-assisted analysis work
**Frame as:** Someone who has applied transformation in a real SME with real constraints
**De-emphasise:** Pure hospitality operations detail
**Key language:** Digital maturity, process digitalisation, automation, AI adoption, SME digitalization

### Revenue Strategy
**Lead with:** Majestic Revenue Management Internship + Dashboard project (sales/revenue focus) + Product Matching/Cost Analysis
**Frame as:** Someone with real revenue data experience and commercial awareness
**De-emphasise:** CRM, content/design work
**Key language:** Revenue management, yield, F&B revenue, pricing, commercial strategy, profitability

### Hospitality Operations
**Lead with:** All hospitality experience — early foundation, Majestic spa, Majestic revenue, Rocxi Beach, Riyadh
**Frame as:** Someone with deep operational hospitality experience who also brings data and digital skills
**De-emphasise:** Abstract digital transformation theory
**Key language:** Guest experience, service quality, hospitality operations, F&B, reservations, luxury standards

## Cross-Role Tailoring Logic

**When a role is a mix of two types** (e.g. CRM + Operations, or Revenue + BI), tell Claude which two angle files to read, and ask it to blend. The dominant angle should determine what leads.

**When a role is in a non-hospitality sector** (e.g. SaaS, professional services), the hospitality experience should still be mentioned — but framed as operational credibility and customer sensibility, not as the main story.

**When a role emphasises a specific tool** (e.g. Salesforce, HubSpot, Tableau), acknowledge the tool difference honestly and position the closest equivalent experience: CoverManager → CRM experience; Looker Studio → BI/dashboard experience. Do not claim proficiency in tools not used.

**When a role is highly technical** (e.g. data engineering, Python, SQL), do not overreach. Carla's profile is operational analyst + digital transformation, not data science. Position around the analytical layer and practical tools, and be honest about where technical depth ends.

## What Makes a Cover Letter Work vs. Fail

| Works | Fails |
|---|---|
| Names the company and role specifically | Generic opening, company name pasted in but nothing else personalised |
| Uses the JD's own language | Uses Carla's language while ignoring the JD's terminology |
| Shows one specific relevant project | Lists everything in the profile without prioritising |
| Makes the combination argument clearly | Tries to be either purely operational or purely technical |
| Confident, specific close | Apologetic, vague close |
| 3–4 tight paragraphs | 5+ paragraphs with padding |

## What Makes a CV Work vs. Fail

| Works | Fails |
|---|---|
| Right experience leads for this role type | Everything listed in the same order for every application |
| Bullets start with action verbs and include outcomes | Bullets describe duties, not impact |
| Keywords from the JD appear naturally | Keywords from the JD are absent |
| 1 page, clean, readable | 2 pages of everything Carla has ever done |
| Headline matches the role | Generic headline on every application |
