# Cover Letter Structure Guide — Carla Manzano Ramells

All cover letters follow this structure. Adapt content using the relevant role angle file and opening hooks file. Length: 3–4 focused paragraphs. No padding.

---

## Structure

### Paragraph 1: Opening Hook
One strong sentence that creates immediate relevance. Connect Carla's background directly to this company and this role. Do not open with "I am applying for..."

Select or adapt from `templates/cover_letter/opening_hooks.md`.

**What it must do:**
- Name the specific role and company
- Immediately signal the most relevant angle
- Create curiosity or resonance — not just a statement of intent

### Paragraph 2: Why This Is Relevant
2–3 sentences connecting Carla's most relevant experience directly to what the role requires.

**Structure:**
- What she has done that is directly relevant (specific, not vague)
- What this means in the context of the role
- One concrete example or project if space allows

**Rules:**
- Be specific. Name a project, a tool, a result.
- Mirror the language of the job description.
- Do not list everything — focus on the 2–3 things that matter most for this role.

### Paragraph 3: The Combination Argument
1–2 sentences explaining why the combination of Carla's operational + analytical + digital background is particularly relevant for this company or role.

This is her competitive advantage. A purely technical candidate does not have the operational intuition. A purely operational candidate does not have the data and digital skills. Carla has both.

**Optional:** Include a sentence showing company knowledge — why this specific company appeals, what she knows about their work, how her profile fits their context.

### Paragraph 4: Closing
Select from `templates/cover_letter/closing_statements.md`.

**Rules:**
- Confident, not apologetic
- Express interest in a conversation
- No hollow phrases like "I look forward to hearing from you at your earliest convenience"

---

## Format Rules

- **Length:** 3–4 paragraphs. Never more than one page.
- **Tone:** Professional but human. Not stiff. Not informal.
- **Language:** Mirror the company's tone where possible — if they use direct, clear language, use the same.
- **Name the role:** Always use the exact job title from the posting.
- **Name the company:** Always personalise. No generic cover letters.
- **No filler:** "I am a passionate and motivated individual" is wasted space. Use specifics instead.
- **File name:** `CL_Carla_Manzano_[CompanyName]_[RoleName].pdf`
