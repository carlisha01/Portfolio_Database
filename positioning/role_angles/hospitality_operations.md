# Role Angle: Hospitality Operations

Use this file when applying to roles such as: Hotel Operations Analyst, F&B Operations Coordinator, Restaurant Operations Manager, Hospitality Operations Support, Hospitality Technology Analyst, Guest Experience Analyst, Revenue and Operations roles within hotels or hospitality groups.

---

## Core Pitch (3 sentences)

I have worked directly inside hospitality operations — from family restaurant service to luxury hotel environments — and I understand what running a service business really involves at every level. What differentiates my profile is that I combine this operational foundation with analytical and digital skills: dashboards, KPI frameworks, CRM workflows, automation, and AI-assisted analysis applied to real hospitality problems. I am looking for an operations role in hospitality where I can contribute both frontline service understanding and the structured, data-informed thinking to improve how the business operates.

---

## What to Foreground

- Early hospitality operational foundation — years inside real service environments, customer-facing skills, pressure management
- Majestic Hotel Group Spa Receptionist — five-star luxury standards, guest interaction, discretion
- Majestic Hotel Group Revenue Management Internship — hotel performance data, F&B income, cross-property reporting
- Rocxi Beach operations — table flow, reservations, service rhythm, team coordination, client experience
- Restaurant Operations Dashboard — operational KPIs, peak hours, revenue visibility
- Customer Feedback Analysis — turning guest feedback into operational improvement signals
- CoverManager CRM — reservations, customer profiles, satisfaction tracking
- Personalised New Year's Eve experience — guest experience design and emotional intelligence
- International Riyadh experience — luxury / VVIP service standards, international adaptability

## What to Downplay

- Technical automation detail (mention briefly as a modern skill)
- EAE programme digital transformation theory (keep brief, frame as practical)
- AI detail (mention as a practical tool, not as a tech angle)

---

## Language to Use

- "Direct experience in restaurant and hotel operations across multiple environments"
- "Worked in five-star hospitality at Majestic Hotel Group — spa reception and revenue management"
- "Managed guest-facing service during high-volume periods"
- "Understand the full service cycle from reservation to post-visit communication"
- "Developed operational dashboards to give management visibility over service performance"
- "Used CoverManager for reservations, customer profiles, and satisfaction tracking"
- "Designed a personalised guest experience for high-expectation clients"
- "Exposure to luxury and VVIP hospitality standards in an international context"
- "Combine operational experience with data and digital tools to improve service decision-making"

## Language to Avoid

- Business analysis jargon as the primary framing
- Overemphasising digital/tech angle (it should complement, not replace, the operational story)
- Framing the Riyadh experience negatively — keep it factual and professional

---

## ATS Keywords to Include

hospitality operations, restaurant operations, hotel operations, F&B operations, front of house, guest experience, customer service, luxury hospitality, VVIP, revenue management, reservations, CoverManager, hospitality technology, service quality, guest satisfaction, KPI, operational reporting, team coordination
