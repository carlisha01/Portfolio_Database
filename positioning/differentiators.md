# Key Differentiators — Carla Manzano Ramells

What makes Carla's profile stand out from other junior candidates targeting business analysis, operations, and digital transformation roles.

---

## 1. Real Operational Background

Carla has not only studied hospitality. She has lived the daily reality of a restaurant business and worked inside real service environments at multiple levels — family restaurant operations, luxury hotel spa, revenue management, VVIP private F&B. This gives her a practical understanding of business problems that many junior candidates, coming directly from academic settings, do not yet have.

When she looks at a dashboard or a CRM export, she is not only seeing data. She is seeing the operational context behind that data: the service moment, the team under pressure, the customer decision, the management challenge. That perspective is rare and valuable.

---

## 2. Combination of Three Worlds in One Profile

Most junior candidates sit clearly in one category: hospitality operations OR data/analytics OR tech/digital tools. Carla sits at the intersection of all three:
- Hospitality operations (restaurant, hotel, luxury F&B)
- Business analysis and data (dashboards, KPIs, revenue data, product matching, CRM exports)
- Practical digital transformation (automation, AI-assisted workflows, SME digitalization)

This combination is increasingly what modern operations, digital transformation, and business analysis roles need — someone who understands the business from the ground up and can also apply data and digital tools to it.

---

## 3. Practical, Business-Validated AI Use

Carla uses AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) as a working tool — for data cleaning, structuring information, categorising feedback, designing workflows, writing and refining scripts, and supporting business analysis. She does not treat AI as a magic answer or present AI outputs without review.

Her approach: AI generates the structure or first pass; she applies business judgment, validates against operational reality, and adapts. This is the right way to use AI in a business context, and she can explain it clearly — which matters in roles involving AI adoption.

---

## 4. Real SME Digital Transformation Experience

Many candidates discuss digital transformation at a corporate theory level. Carla has applied transformation thinking to a real SME with real constraints: a family restaurant with limited time, messy data, informal processes, a busy team, and genuine operational problems to solve.

This means she understands what transformation looks like when the business cannot pause operations, when data is in spreadsheets and email attachments, and when any solution must be simple enough for a non-technical team to actually use. This is exactly the experience that SME-focused roles, hospitality technology roles, and junior digital transformation consulting positions value.

## 5. Strong Customer Experience Sensibility

Because of her hospitality background, Carla brings a human layer to analysis. She understands that behind every review, reservation, cancellation, complaint, or dashboard metric, there is a customer experience and an operational decision. This customer sensibility is an advantage in CRM roles, client success roles, customer experience analysis, and any context where data needs to be translated back into human behaviour.
