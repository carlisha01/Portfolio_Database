About
CMR Carla Manzano Ramells

Operations meet data.
Patterns become strategy.

Hospitality taught me that the real story is never on the surface. After years working inside operational environments — on the floor, inside the detail, watching every moving part — I became genuinely obsessed with understanding why things happen, not just that they do. That curiosity pulled me toward data: building dashboards to surface patterns management couldn't see, automating the work eating up hours each week, and eventually designing the systems that connect what happens on the ground to what shows up in the numbers.

Every project here started with a real problem in a real environment — not a dataset downloaded for a course. The work sits at the intersection of operations, analytics, and technology, built by someone who has worked at every level of an operation.

A constant thread: if there's something I need to build, I figure out how. Apps Script, Looker Studio, AI workflows — none of it was handed to me. I learned each one because a problem demanded it, and that habit of teaching myself whatever's needed hasn't slowed down.

Portfolio — Section 5

Automation & Process Optimisation

If I'm doing the same thing twice, I want to find a way to stop. These projects came from that instinct — identifying where manual work was eating time and building something to take it away permanently.

Problem

Manual Weekly Reports

Management spent 3+ hours each Monday consolidating sales, reservation, and feedback data from separate sources into a single report.

Solution

Automated Report Collection

Google Apps Script pulls data from multiple Sheets on a schedule, consolidates it, and emails a formatted summary report to management automatically every Monday morning.

Problem

Scattered Supplier Invoices

Supplier invoices arrived via email, WhatsApp, and paper — making cost tracking slow, error-prone, and impossible to analyse in aggregate.

Solution

Gmail-to-Drive Automation

Apps Script monitors a dedicated Gmail inbox, extracts invoice attachments, renames them with supplier and date metadata, and deposits them into structured Drive folders automatically.

Problem

CSV Processing Overhead

POS system exports required manual cleaning and formatting before they could be used in dashboards — a repetitive 45-minute task performed weekly.

Solution

CSV Processing Pipeline

Automated pipeline that detects new CSV exports, standardises column names, removes noise rows, formats dates, and loads clean data into the reporting sheet — reducing the task to zero manual effort.

The Approach

How I Think About Work

Every project here came from a real problem that needed solving — not an exercise, not a demo dataset. Real constraints, real pressure, something that had to work on Monday morning.

01
Business Thinking

Operations drive numbers, and numbers should guide operations. Every analysis is grounded in what the business actually needs to decide — not what is technically interesting.

02
Operational Understanding

Time on the ground informs every spreadsheet. Understanding why a metric behaves a certain way requires knowing what actually happens when things get busy.

03
Data as a Tool

Clean data, honest models, and actionable insights. The goal is clarity, not complexity — the best analysis is the one a busy manager can act on in three minutes.

04
AI as Augmentation

I've taught myself every tool in this portfolio because a specific problem demanded it. That approach — find the problem first, learn the solution second — shapes how I use AI too: with purpose, not novelty.
