# Professional Narrative — Carla Manzano Ramells

Carla's professional story is not linear in the traditional sense, but it is coherent and increasingly focused.

---

## The Foundation: Operations From the Inside

Carla's professional identity started being shaped long before any formal job title. Growing up around her family restaurant, Rocxi Beach, she learned what it actually means to run a service business: the operational pressure of a busy service, the importance of reading customers correctly, the gap between what looks smooth from the outside and what is actually happening in the kitchen and front of house. This was not theoretical. It was daily, real, and demanding.

This early exposure gave her something many junior candidates lack: genuine operational intuition. She did not study hospitality and then try to imagine what it feels like. She lived it, and that experience became the foundation of everything that followed.

---

## Building Structure: Education and Formal Hospitality Experience

When Carla began her BA in Tourism and Hotel Management at IQS School of Management (Ramon Llull University), she was giving academic structure to experience she already had. The degree deepened her understanding of hospitality economics, customer experience design, revenue management, and the business systems behind service operations.

During this period she worked at Majestic Hotel Group in two distinct roles. As a spa receptionist, she experienced luxury hospitality from a guest-facing perspective — five-star standards, discretion, personal attention, composure under the expectations of high-end clients. Later, a revenue management internship opened the analytical side of hospitality: daily cross-property data, reservations and F&B income, Excel-based reporting, AI-assisted data structuring, and the commercial logic behind hotel performance. That internship made her realise how much she was drawn to understanding operations through information, not just through instinct.

---

## The Pivot: From Operational to Analytical and Digital

The clearest shift in Carla's trajectory came when she began combining her operational experience with digital tools and analytical thinking. Through her Digital Transformation for SMEs programme at EAE Business School, she developed a full transformation roadmap for Rocxi Beach — a real SME with real constraints, not a case study. This meant process mapping, CRM analysis, automation identification, KPI design, and dashboard planning — all grounded in an environment she knew from the inside.

In parallel, she began building practical digital projects: a restaurant operations dashboard in Looker Studio and Power BI; an AI-assisted customer feedback categorisation system; a Gmail-to-Drive automation using Google Apps Script to manage recurring CoverManager reports; a product matching and cost analysis connecting sales data with supplier purchase data; and a CRM segmentation strategy for making the restaurant's customer database an active business tool.

Each of these projects came from a real operational problem she had observed directly. None of them were academic exercises.

---

## International Experience: A Step Into Complexity

In January 2026, Carla moved to Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, for a planned 12-month role in a luxury hospitality / VVIP private environment. This was a significant step: moving internationally, entering a very high-standard environment, and adapting to a new culture and professional context.

She returned to Barcelona in March 2026 due to escalating regional conditions. The experience, while shorter than planned, reinforced her adaptability, her ability to step into unfamiliar high-expectation environments, and the maturity required to make a complex professional decision. It also helped clarify her direction: she is not looking for the most prestigious setting, but for the right context — one where operations, data, and digital tools can create real business value.

---

## Where She Is Now: Building for What Comes Next

Carla is currently building a portfolio that demonstrates practical business projects, completing her degree (graduation ceremony July 2026), and actively applying to junior roles in business analysis, operations analysis, digital transformation, CRM, revenue strategy, and AI-assisted process improvement.

Her professional value is not about being the most technical person in the room. It is about being the person who understands the business problem, learns the tools needed to address it, structures the information clearly, and helps organisations move from operational confusion to better decisions.

That is the through-line of her entire profile: operational grounding, analytical curiosity, practical digital skills, and a strong sense of what business information is actually for.
