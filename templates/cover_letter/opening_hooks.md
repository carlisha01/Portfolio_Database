# Cover Letter Opening Hooks — Carla Manzano Ramells

Strong opening sentences by role type. Never start with “I am writing to apply for” or “I am a motivated professional.” Select and adapt — always insert the specific company and role name.

---

## Business Analyst Roles

> My interest in business analysis did not start with a spreadsheet course — it started inside a running restaurant, where I began to see how much value structured data could bring to decisions that were being made entirely on instinct.

> What draws me to the [Role] at [Company] is the chance to connect structured analysis with real operational context — which is exactly what I have been building toward through my revenue management internship at Majestic Hotel Group and my practical projects in restaurant performance and customer feedback analysis.

> At Majestic Hotel Group I learned that the most valuable thing a business analyst can do is turn messy data into a decision — and at Rocxi Beach I built the projects that proved I could do that in practice.

---

## Operations Analyst / Business Operations Roles

> I have spent years working inside hospitality operations and the last two building the analytical layer on top of that experience — which is why the [Role] at [Company] fits exactly where I am headed.

> Most operations analysts understand processes from a diagram. I understand them from the inside, which is why my dashboards, automation projects, and process improvement work are grounded in operational realities rather than theoretical frameworks.

> The [Role] at [Company] caught my attention because it requires someone who can bridge the gap between how a business actually runs and how it should be documented, measured, and improved — that bridge is what I have been building.

## Digital Transformation Roles

> I developed a digital transformation roadmap for a real SME — not a case study — and what that experience taught me is that transformation only works when it is built around how the business actually operates, not around the technology itself.

> The [Role] at [Company] interests me because [Company] is working on [relevant area] — and I have spent the last two years building practical transformation experience in exactly that kind of environment: real constraints, real teams, real problems that needed digital solutions.

> Most digital transformation candidates can tell you what AI or automation can do. I can tell you what it takes to make those tools work inside a business where the team is busy, the data is messy, and the solution has to be simple enough to actually stick.

## CRM and Customer Data Roles

> I have worked with CoverManager long enough to know that a CRM database full of customer profiles is only as valuable as the strategy behind it — and designing that strategy is exactly what drew me to the [Role] at [Company].

> Customer data has been central to my work for the past two years: analysing reservation patterns, segmenting client profiles, categorising post-visit feedback, and automating report exports — and I am looking to apply that experience at [Company].

> What excites me about the [Role] at [Company] is the focus on using customer data as a relationship tool, not just a contact list — which is exactly the problem I have been working on at Rocxi Beach.

## Revenue Strategy and Commercial Roles

> My first real understanding of revenue management came from my internship at Majestic Hotel Group in Barcelona, where I worked daily with cross-property reservations, revenue, and F&B income data — and it is that experience that makes the [Role] at [Company] a natural next step.

> Revenue is not just a number — it is the result of a series of operational and commercial decisions made under uncertainty, and my experience working with hospitality revenue data has given me a clear appreciation of what drives it and what limits it.

> I have spent time on both sides of revenue: understanding it operationally through restaurant and hotel work, and analytically through data structuring, dashboard development, and cost-margin analysis — and I am looking to deepen that at [Company].

## Hospitality Operations Roles

> Hospitality operations is where my professional identity started — from early frontline experience in a family restaurant to luxury spa service at Majestic Hotel Group to a VVIP private F&B role in Riyadh — and the [Role] at [Company] is exactly the environment where I want to apply everything I have built since.

> I understand what a five-star guest experience actually requires because I have delivered it directly, and I understand what it costs operationally because I have tracked the data behind it — that combination is what I bring to the [Role] at [Company].

> What makes me different as a hospitality operations candidate is not only the years of service experience — it is the dashboards, CRM strategies, automation tools, and analytical frameworks I have built on top of that experience to make operations run more intelligently.
